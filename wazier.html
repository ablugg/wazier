Wazier's Philosophy

Your mind deserves a private sanctuary.

AI has become extraordinary. But somewhere along the way, your most intimate thoughts · your questions, your fears, your ideas · became data points in someone else's model. Wazier was built on a simple belief: intelligence should serve the person, not surveil them.



Running 100% off the grid, Wazier never sends a single byte of your conversation to any server. Your data is encrypted at rest with industry-standard AES-256, stored only on your machine, and automatically forgotten after 7 days · unless you choose otherwise.

How It Works

Simple to use.
Impossible to compromise.

Wazier wraps the power of Ollama in a calm, focused interface designed to feel as natural as thinking out loud · while keeping every thought sealed away from the world.

01
Install locally, once.

One install on your machine. Wazier pulls Ollama models directly to your drive. No account required. No email. Just open it and begin.

02
Conversations stay local, encrypted.

Every message is encrypted with AES-256 as it's written. Stored in a local vault only your device can open. Nothing leaves.

03
Forget naturally, remember intentionally.

After 7 days, conversations are purged automatically. Pin something to your permanent memory vault · still local, still encrypted, still yours.

04
Feed it documents. It learns forever.

Drop in a PDF, a note, a research paper. Wazier chunks it, embeds it using nomic-embed-text running inside Ollama on your machine, and stores the vectors in an encrypted local file (docs.enc). Every step is a localhost call or in-memory computation. At chat time, your query is embedded the same way, the top matching chunks are retrieved, and injected into context. Your document vault never expires and never leaves your device.

05
Two stores. Two rules. Total clarity.

Wazier maintains two separate encrypted vaults. The chat vault follows the 7-day expiry cycle, conversations purge automatically and leave nothing behind. The document vault is permanent and only grows when you add to it. You control each one independently. There is no overlap, no confusion, and no surprises about what gets kept.
